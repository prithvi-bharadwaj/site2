# Text Compression Plan

## What Shaan Puri does that works

1. **Hook with the weird detail, not the age.** He doesn't say "at 25, I started a podcast." He says "100,000,000 downloads." The specific, unexpected number IS the sentence. Everything else is optional context.

2. **One idea per line.** Short paragraphs. Breathing room. The reader's eye never gets lost in a wall.

3. **Subvert, then explain.** "they thought i was a studio. i was one person with caffeine." The contrast does the work. The explanation is the dessert, not the main course.

4. **Conversational asides earn trust.** "that's not a sentence i expected to type" — you already do this. Do more of it. These lines make the reader feel like they're in on it.

5. **Cut the resume framing.** "at 15, I taught myself..." reads like a LinkedIn timeline. Shaan never dates things unless the date IS the point.

---

## Current structure problem

Every section starts with "at [age], [thing I did]." By the third one, the reader's brain pattern-matches and skims. The age isn't the interesting part. The *detail* is.

## Tone shifts (Shaan-inspired)

- **Kill "at [age]" as a section opener.** Use age as a detail inside the sentence, not the lead. "at 15 i designed hoodies and sold them to my entire school" reads better than "at 15, taught myself graphic design and started a merch store."
- **Lead with the outcome or the absurdity, not the process.** "they thought i was a studio" > "so i taught myself everything"
- **More one-liners.** "that's not a sentence i expected to type." "not my proudest business model." These are already in the copy — protect them, give them room to breathe.
- **Shorter paragraphs everywhere.** If a paragraph has more than 2 sentences, see if the third sentence can be its own line.
